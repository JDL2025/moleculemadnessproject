🧪 Molecule Madness!

Choose your molecules · Build them with gumdrops & toothpicks · Show the world their shapes!

🍬 How to Build Your Molecule

1Gumdrops = Atoms.
Pick a color to represent each element the molecule is made of. You have up to 5 colors!
2Toothpicks = Bonds.
Bonds are the connections between atoms.
3Double Bonds = TWO toothpicks.
Stronger than single bonds. Use two toothpicks side by side to show double bonds.

Take apart your expert molecule. Throw out all the used toothpicks and your color legend notecard. If you want to eat your gumdrops, you may. If not, throw out your entire ziplock bag.

Project Part 3

Hero and Villain Posters!

Every molecule has a story. You're going to tell two of them.

🧬 Project Part 3 Overview

You will create two posters — one for a molecule villain, and one for a molecule hero. You will choose which molecules to feature as your hero and villain, then you'll color and decorate your posters, including written descriptions about what makes each molecule dangerous or amazing.

🔫
Wanted Poster
Pick a molecule that causes harm, danger, or destruction. Put it on trial.
🦸
Hero Poster
Pick a molecule that helps, heals, or saves the day. Give it the glory it deserves.
📌
The Rule: You cannot use the same molecule for both posters. Each poster must feature a different molecule. You may choose any molecule from Part 1 of this project (top of the page)!
🔫
Poster #1

The Wanted Poster

Your molecule is a criminal. Make it look dangerous.

Cover Page
Writing Page
Step 1
Choose Your Criminal Molecule
Pick any molecule from Part 1 of this project that has a dark side — something dangerous, toxic, explosive, or harmful to the environment. Good villain choices include TNT, Sulfur Dioxide, Ozone, Hydrogen Peroxide, or Carbon Dioxide. But any molecule can be a villain if you are bright enough to make the argument! Can you find an unexpected villain molecule that no one else has noticed?
Step 2
Decorate the Cover Page
Color and decorate the "WANTED / Dead or Alive" cover. Draw a colorful picture of your molecule in the big box — you can sketch its shape, draw atoms and bonds, or make it look like a mugshot. Make it dramatic!
Step 3
Fill In the Suspect Name
On the writing page, fill in your molecule's name on the "Suspect Name" line. Include its chemical formula too — for example: Carbon Dioxide (CO₂).
Step 4
Describe your Criminal's Details — 5 to 6 FULL SENTENCES
This is the most important part! In the "Criminal Details" box, write 5–6 complete, descriptive sentences. Use the checklist below — every item marked 🔴 is required. Items marked ⭐ are bonus!
🔴 Required Write your molecule's full name and chemical formula. (for example: "The suspect's name is Sulfur Dioxide, also known as SO₂.") Does your villain go by any aliases or alternate names? Use Google to find out and include them on your poster.
🔴 Required Name the elements it is made of and how many total atoms it has.
🔴 Required Describe its 3D shape (tetrahedral, bent, linear, etc.).
🔴 Required Explain the crime — what harmful or dangerous thing does this molecule do in the real world?
🔴 Required Describe where this molecule is found — where does it hide in nature or everyday life?
⭐ Bonus Name one real-world event or place where this molecule has caused problems.
⭐ Bonus Write a warning to the public — should people be worried about this molecule? How can they stay safe?
Step 5
Attach the Pages Together
Staple or glue your cover page and writing page together. If you used the extra "Criminal Details" continuation page, attach that too.
🦸
Poster #2

The Hero Poster

Your molecule saved the day. Give it the headline it deserves.

Cover Page
Writing Page
Step 1
Choose Your Hero Molecule
Pick any molecule from Part 1 of this project that does something helpful, healing, or life-saving. Great hero choices include Penicillin G, Glucose, Water, Serotonin, Dopamine, or Caffeine. But any molecule can be a hero if you are bright enough to make the argument! Can you find an unexpected hero molecule that no one else has thought of?
Step 2
Decorate the Cover Page
Color and decorate the "HERO ALERT! / Molecule Saves the Day!" newspaper cover. Draw a colorful picture of your molecule in the photo box — make it look like a front page news story. Add color, excitement, and flair!
Step 3
Fill In the Hero Name
On the writing page, write your molecule's name on the "Hero Name" line at the top. Include its chemical formula too — for example: Penicillin G (C₁₆H₁₈N₂O₄S).
Step 4
Describe your Hero's Story — 5 to 6 FULL SENTENCES
This is the most important part! In the "Hero's Story" section, write 5–6 complete, descriptive sentences. Use the checklist below — every item marked 🔴 is required. Items marked ⭐ are bonus!
🔴 Required Write your molecule's full name and chemical formula. (for example: "Meet Penicillin G, also known as C₁₆H₁₈N₂O₄S — one of the greatest discoveries in human history.") Does your hero go by any other names? Use Google to find out and include them on your poster.
🔴 Required Name the elements it is made of and how many total atoms it has.
🔴 Required Describe its 3D shape (tetrahedral, bent, linear, etc.).
🔴 Required Explain the superpower — what amazing, helpful, or life-saving thing does this molecule do?
🔴 Required Describe the origin story — how was this molecule discovered or first used by humans?
⭐ Bonus Give a specific example of how this molecule has helped people, animals, or the planet.
⭐ Bonus Write a "Why Berwyn Celebrates" sentence — how might YOUR life or community be better because of this molecule?
Step 5
Attach the Pages Together
Staple or glue your cover page and writing page together. If you used the extra "Hero's Story" continuation page, attach that too.
⭐ Choose your molecules wisely — and tell their stories well! ⭐

Design Your Own Molecule Trading Card!

Pick any molecule · Draw it · Make up its stats · Challenge your friends

📋 Get this sheet from your teacher!

What's the activity?

Get a trading card sheet from your teacher and design your very own molecule trading card — like a Pokémon card, but for chemistry! Pick any molecule from this page, draw it on the card, and fill it with science stats and creative details. When you're done, compare cards with classmates. Whose molecule would win in a fight?

Your card must include all 4 of these:

1Molecule Name + Formula — the full name and chemical formula (e.g. Caffeine · C₈H₁₀N₄O₂)
2A Drawing of Your Molecule — sketch its shape in the image box. Use the 3D viewer on this page for reference!
3Superpower or Weakness — what amazing or dangerous thing does this molecule do in the real world?
4Your Stats — invent an Attack, Defense, and Special Ability score (1–100) and explain why you chose them!
💡 Bonus challenge: Make a card for your villain molecule AND your hero molecule, then figure out who would win in a head-to-head battle. Write a one-sentence battle description on the back!
🌟 Curiosity is the best superpower. Keep exploring! 🌟
